Our Beloved India

India is a land of rich and diverse culture and heritage, where over 140 crores of people live. It is an unique country where people of different religions, nature and climate live together. Though each and every person is unique here, in any necessity they are always able to unite themselves and show the true meaning of 'Unity In Diversity' .

Our National Anthem is composed by the greatest of all, Kaviguru, Ravindranath Tagore. Our National Song has been composed by another great person of our motherland, Bankimchandra Chatterji.

Tourist Spots of India

States

Andhra Pradesh

Tirupathi Temple

Many Hindu festivals like Ugadi, Krishna Janmashtami, Maha Shivratri etc are held between months of September and October. Many temples like the Venkateswara Temple, Govindaraja Temple, Padmavathi Temple etc. are the ornaments of Andhra Pradesh.

Worship of Stalagmite Lingam inside the Borra Caves

Andhra Pradesh has several rock-cut caves like Undavalli Caves, Belum Caves.

The valleys and hills include, Araku Valley, Horsley Hills, Papi Hills etc. Arma Konda peak located in Visakhapatnam district is the highest peak in Eastern Ghats.

Arunachal Pradesh

Tawang Monastry, Arunachal Pradesh

Tawang receives snowfall every year during December–January. There is also a ski lift in town.

The Tawan Monastry is the birthplace of the 6th Dalai Lama.

The Sela Pass, Arunachal Pradesh

There are many more tourist attractions in Arunachal Pradesh like Bumla, Lumla, Shonga -tser (Madhuri) Lake, PTSO Lake etc.

Nyokum Festival

Nyokum is the festival of the Nyishi tribe and is celebrated for the harmony and prosperity of the people. Nyokum Festival is celebrated on the 28th of February every year in East Kemang district, Lower Subansiri district, Kurung Kummey district and Papumpar district. There are many other fastivals celebrated here like Siang River Festival, Pangsau Pass Winter Festival, Ziro Festival of Music etc.

Assam

The kaziranga National Park in Assam is very popular for One Horned Rhinoceros

The Kaziranga National Park in the Golaghat, Karbi Anglong and Nagaon districts of the state of Assam, India, hosts two-thirds of the world's great one-horned rhinoceroses and is a World Heritage Site. In this sanctuary many other animals like tigers, elephants, wild water buffalo, and swamp deer etc. are also found.

Assam Tea Assam Tea Garden

Assam is one of the biggest and most famous tea stocks of India as well as the world.

Bohag Bihu is a very famous harvest festival in Assam

Bihu is a set of three important Assamese festivals in the Indian state of Assam — Rongali or Bohag Bihu observed in April, Kongali or Kati Bihu observed in October, and Bhogali or Magh Bihu observed in January.
